About

Hi, I’m Dr. Mayra I. Rodríguez, an urban forester who helps cities, organizations, and communities use data, education, and collaboration to build healthier urban forests.

My work sits at the intersection of urban forestry and community planning. I partner with municipalities, nonprofits, and community groups to better understand tree canopy, climate risk, and nature-based solutions—and translate that knowledge into practical strategies, tools, and programs.

My work focuses on:

Urban forest planning
Tree canopy & geospatial analysis
Green space planning & design
Questionnaire development
Community workshops
Youth engagement & co-design
Participatory Action Research
Translating science into practical tools

Whether facilitating community conversations, analyzing spatial data, or designing learning experiences, my goal is the same: helping communities make informed decisions about their urban forests and environmental future.

This site highlights examples of my projects and products. If you're interested in collaborating or bringing urban forestry initiatives to your community, don't hesitate to reach out.
